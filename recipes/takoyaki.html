Takoyaki

A yummy ball snack from Japan!

The 1930s saw the creation of takoyaki, which is still renowned as being Osakan food today. Octopus is the "tako" in "takoyaki," and "yaki" is baked. A ball-shaped snack composed with batter based on wheat, and may have a variety of different toppings and fillings.

The main filling is octopus, as you might have anticipated given that the word tako in takoyaki means octopus! Green onion, pickled ginger, and tempura remnants typically make up the remaining fillings. The takoyaki is then covered with bonito flakes, dried laver, Japanese mayonnaise, and takoyaki sauce, which is quite reminiscent of Worcester sauce. But there are several distinct differences! For instance, you can find ones with shrimp or other types of seafood inside!

Ingredients

  • 450g dashi batter
  • 100g chopped boiled octopus
  • 30g pickled red ginger
  • 30g green onions
  • 40g tempura scraps

Steps

  1. Apply generous ammount of oil in the takoyaki pan.
  2. Pour the batter until the edge of the hole.
  3. Put the octopus, pickled red ginger, green onions, and tempura scraps according to your liking.
  4. When the bottom of the takoyaki is crispy flip and rotate it with a screwer or chopsticks vertically.
  5. Add some batter and let it cook until crispy.
  6. Add some sauce to the cooked takoyaki. (optional)
